# Vision

## Problem

Finding people to play board games with - beyond your existing friend group - is fragmented today: scattered Facebook groups, Meetup.com listing, BGG fouurm threads, and local store mailing lists that don't talk to each other. There's no single place to say "I own these games, I like these kind of games, I'm free Tuesday evenings, and I'd like to meet people nearby who feel the same way."

## Goal

This project has two goals:

1. **Learning goal**: build hands-on experience with the DevOps toolchain - Docker, service decomposition, AWS fundamentals, Kubernetes, infrastructure as code, CI/CD, and observability - by applying it to something real enough to have actual architectural decisions
2. **Product goal**: build a genuinley usefull small social platform for board games to find people, form groups, and organize events - first for personal/local use, but designed like it could plausbily grow.

## Target Users

- **Hobbyist players** who want to meet new people locally to play games with beyond people they already know.
- **Casual organizers** who run a regular game night and want a lightweight way to manage who's coming, without a group chat spiraling out of control.
- **Clubs and local businesses** (board game cafes, hobby stores) who want to host larger, recurring, or public events and reach people beyond their existing mailing list.

## Explicit Non-goals (for now)

- Not building real-time chat/messaging - RSVP and posts are enough for now.
- Not a native mobile app - web-first.
- Not handling payments/ticketing for events, even though businesses might eventually want that.
- Not trying to out-feature existing social networks - the "feed" exists to support finding groups and games, not to be a general-purpose social app.
